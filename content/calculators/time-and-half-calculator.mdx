## Part 1: How to Calculate Time and a Half (The Exact Formula)

The phrase "time and a half" is the bedrock of American labor law. The federal Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) mandates that covered nonexempt employees must receive overtime pay for any hours worked over 40 in a single, fixed workweek. The minimum rate for this overtime is "time and one-half" of their regular rate of pay.

### The Basic Math Formula
If you want to know **how to calculate time and a half**, the formula is incredibly simple:
`Regular Hourly Rate × 1.5 = Overtime Hourly Rate`

Once you have your Overtime Hourly Rate, you multiply it by the number of overtime hours you worked:
`Overtime Hourly Rate × Overtime Hours = Total Overtime Premium`

**Real World Example:**
Let's assume you work at a warehouse making **$20.00 an hour**. This week, you worked **50 hours**.
1. **Regular Pay:** Your first 40 hours are paid at your base rate. (40 hrs × $20 = **$800**)
2. **Calculate the Overtime Rate:** You multiply your $20 base rate by 1.5 to get your time-and-a-half rate. ($20 × 1.5 = **$30.00/hr**)
3. **Calculate the Overtime Premium:** You worked 10 hours of overtime. (10 hrs × $30 = **$300**)
4. **Total Gross Pay:** Add your regular pay and your overtime premium. ($800 + $300 = **$1,100**)

## Part 2: Time and a Half Calculator for Salaried Employees

One of the biggest misconceptions in the modern workforce is that salaried employees do not get overtime. This is categorically false. Under the FLSA, unless you meet specific "exempt" criteria (often related to executive management, specialized administrative duties, or a high salary threshold), you are legally entitled to time and a half—even if you are paid a fixed annual salary.

### How the Salary Converter Works
To calculate time and a half for a salaried worker, you first have to deduce what their "effective" hourly rate is. Our calculator features a dedicated toggle for Salaried Employees.

1. **Enter Your Annual Salary:** Let's say you make **$65,000** a year.
2. **Find the Weekly Rate:** The engine divides your salary by 52 weeks to find your gross weekly pay ($1,250/week).
3. **Find the Hourly Rate:** It then divides that weekly pay by the standard 40 hours to find your Effective Hourly Rate (**$31.25/hr**).
4. **Calculate the Overtime Rate:** Your time and a half rate is now established at **$46.88/hr**.

If you are a salaried worker putting in 60-hour weeks without receiving that $46.88/hr premium, you need to speak to your HR department immediately to confirm your exemption status.

## Part 3: Double Time vs. Time and a Half

While time and a half (1.5x) is the federal standard, many situations call for even higher premium pay rates. Our calculator features a dropdown menu allowing you to switch between multipliers to handle these specific scenarios.

### Double Time (2.0x)
There is no federal law requiring double time. However, in states like California, the law is far more aggressive. In California, if you work more than 12 hours in a single day, or if you work more than 8 hours on your seventh consecutive day of work in a single workweek, your employer must pay you **Double Time**. 
- Formula: `Base Rate × 2.0 = Double Time Rate`

### Double Time and a Half Calculator (2.5x)
This massive multiplier is almost exclusively used for Premium Holiday Pay. Many union contracts, hospital networks, and municipal governments offer this to employees willing to work on major holidays like Thanksgiving or Christmas.
- You receive your standard "Holiday Pay" for the day (1.0x).
- Plus, you receive time-and-a-half for the hours you actually physically worked (1.5x).
- Totaling a massive **2.5x multiplier** for the day.
