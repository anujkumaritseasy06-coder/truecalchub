## Estimating Bulk Sand Tonnage

Sand is one of the most versatile materials in construction and landscaping. Whether you are building a backyard oasis for your children, leveling a heavy above-ground pool, or screeding a flawless bed for a brick patio, accurately estimating your sand requirement is critical.

## Understanding Sand Density

You cannot order sand by the square foot. Like all heavy aggregates, sand is sold by the **Ton**.

While heavy crushed driveway stone weighs 2,800 pounds per cubic yard, sand is slightly lighter. The industry standard density for dry/damp sand is **2,600 pounds (1.3 Tons) per Cubic Yard**. 

Our calculation engine handles this conversion automatically:
1. Calculates the geometric volume in Cubic Feet.
2. Converts Cubic Feet to Cubic Yards.
3. Multiplies the Cubic Yards by **1.3** to output the exact required Tonnage.

## The Compaction Factor: To Tamp or Not To Tamp?

The most critical decision when ordering sand is determining your **Compaction Factor**. 

Sand is highly compressible. When you pour it out of a wheelbarrow, it is loose and full of air. If you run a heavy vibratory plate compactor over it, the air is squeezed out, and the sand level will visibly sink.

* **0% Compaction (Loose):** Use this setting for children's sandboxes or beach volleyball courts. You want the sand to remain soft and fluffy. You do not want extra material.
* **10% Compaction (Standard Base):** Use this setting if you are laying sand as a base for a patio, retaining wall, or above-ground pool. You *will* be compacting this sand, meaning it will shrink. If you don't order an extra 10% to account for that shrinkage, you will run short of material.

## The Three Types of Construction Sand

Do not make the mistake of buying the wrong type of sand for your project.

### 1. Mason Sand (Fine)
Mason sand is heavily washed and screened to remove all pebbles. It is incredibly fine and smooth. 
* **Best For:** Children's sandboxes, mixing mortar for bricklaying, and lining the base of above-ground pools (to prevent vinyl punctures).

### 2. Concrete Sand (Coarse)
Concrete sand is coarse and jagged. It contains small pebbles and grit.
* **Best For:** Mixing structural concrete, and acting as the 1-inch leveling "screed" layer beneath brick pavers. Because it is coarse, it locks together better than mason sand under a patio.

### 3. Polymeric Sand (Jointing)
This is a highly specialized, expensive retail product sold in small buckets or bags. It contains chemical polymers.
* **Best For:** Sweeping into the cracks between finished brick pavers. When you spray it with a hose, the polymers activate and turn the sand into a hard, weed-proof mortar. **Never** use this as a base layer.

## The Retail Bag Trap

Because sand is often used for small backyard projects, homeowners frequently attempt to buy it at big-box hardware stores. 

Retail bags usually weigh 50 pounds. It takes exactly **40 bags to equal one ton.** 

If you are building a 10x10 foot sandbox at 8 inches deep, our calculator will show that you need **3.2 Tons** of sand. That is the equivalent of **128 individual 50lb bags**. At $5 per bag retail, that is over $640. 

A local landscape yard will deliver 3.2 tons of bulk sand to your driveway for roughly $150 to $200 total. Our calculator actively monitors your bag count and will trigger a financial warning if your project exceeds 40 bags, strongly advising you to pivot to bulk delivery.
